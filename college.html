College

I'm a rising junior in Winthrop House at Harvard University, intending to graduate in 2023 with a master's degree (A.M.) in Linguistics and a joint bachelor's degree (A.B.) in Comparative Literature and Mathematics. I'm also pursuing a secondary field in Ethnicity, Migration, Rights and a foreign language citation in French. Here, you'll find a short summary of what I've been up to at Harvard! Feel free to check out my courses, activities and work, awards and fellowships, and theatre résumé!

Summer 2019

I was enrolled in COMP S-107: Migrations and Boundaries: Reconceptualizing Mobility in the Eastern Mediterranean and Beyond at Harvard's Center for Hellenic Studies in Nafplio and Thessaloniki, Greece. This course consisted of eight interlinked seminars:

  • Borders, Mobility, and Migration in Historical Perspective (Nicolas Prevelakis)
  • Greece and its History: Empires, Nations, Migrations (Dimitris Kastritsis)
  • Crossings of Boundaries between social order and disorder in the East Mediterranean, from the Bronze Age to the era of Alexander (Gregory Nagy)
  • Rome and China: Boundaries, identities and outsiders before the nation state (Emma Dench and Michael Puett)
  • Mobility and Identity in the Byzantine World (Dimiter Angelov)
  • Via Egnatia: Mobility, Cultural Geographies, and Material Culture (Eurydice Georganteli)
  • Migrants, Exiles, and Refugees in the Modern Eastern Mediterranean (Ilham Khuri-Makdisi)
  • Hospitality and Displacement (Yota Batsaki)
