About Me

Born in Hong Kong and raised in Richmond Hill, Ontario, I currently split my time between Greater Toronto and Greater Boston. I'm a musician, theatre-maker, linguist, and multioriented researcher currently pursuing a master's degree in Linguistics and a bachelor's degree in Comparative Literature and Mathematics, with minors in Computer Science and French, at Harvard University (if you want to know more about my college career, click here).

Though my interests are very widespread, I find myself particularly drawn to the intersection between effective, creative communication and equitable representation. I'm interested in understanding the ways in which we humans are able to tell stories and interact with one another, across all sorts of creative and analytical media — and how these are ultimately influenced and mediated by our identities and our culture. The research that I undertake and the art that I create serve to grapple with different facets of this complex idea. With them, I hope to:

  • stress the necessity of showcasing and celebrating underrepresented voices in the arts and in research;
  • remind us of the crucial role of art-making in community-building, from its ability to ask critical questions to its unique power to inspire empathy; and
  • contribute to multidisciplinary discourse in a world that is becoming increasingly stratified.

In music, you'll find me primarily as a composer. My compositions have been performed across Canada and the United States by ensembles including the Harvard-Radcliffe Collegium Musicum, Etobicoke Philharmonic Orchestra, Scarborough Philharmonic Orchestra, and Sneak Peek Orchestra. I'm also a conductor, vocalist, pianist, and (less often) a violinist and percussionist.

In theatre, I'm an avid music director and composer of musical theatre, though I've also served as a finance producer. I've worked professionally as a music director and orchestrator for the American Repertory Theater and have had projects sponsored by the Office of the Arts at Harvard. I've also taken part in the Writers' Workshop at Toronto's New Music Theatre Intensives.

In research, I'm primarily interested in linguistics (especially historical linguistics, sociolinguistics, and pragmatics) and literature (especially contemporary Anglophone and Francophone Asian-diasporic poetry and drama). I'm a Carol K. Pforzheimer Fellow at the Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study and a member of Harvard's Meaning and Modality Linguistics Laboratory.

In my free time, I love learning languages! I'm fluent in English and French and proficient in Cantonese and Mandarin; I also have reading knowledge of Spanish, and am working on my German and Italian! I love trying new food, with a special penchant for dessert and even more special penchant for fresh-squeezed orange juice. Beyond that, you can probably find me reading, singing, or playing Nintendo video games.
